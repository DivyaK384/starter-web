Introduction
By the end of the 3rd century, the powerful
empires established by the Kushanas in the
north and Satavahanas in the south had
lost their greatness and strength. After the
decline of Kushanas and Satavahanas,
Chandragupta carved out a kingdom and
establish his dynastic rule, which lasted
for about two hundred years. After the
downfall of the Guptas and thereafter and
interregnum of nearly 50 years, Harsha of
Vardhana dynasty ruled North India from
606 to 647 A.D (CE).
